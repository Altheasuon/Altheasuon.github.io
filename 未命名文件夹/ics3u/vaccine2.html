The development history of vaccines

time event
10th century BC China used nasal inhalation to inoculate pox (smallpox virus) to prevent smallpox.
15th century Turks and Arabs inoculated patients with pox through the nose to prevent smallpox.
1796 British doctor Edward Jenner successfully used cowpox virus to inoculate humans for the first time to prevent smallpox, which was the beginning of modern vaccines.
1885 French scientist Louis Pasteur successfully prepared a rabies vaccine, the first time a vaccine was used to prevent animal infectious diseases.
1921 French scientist Calmet used the BCG vaccine for the first time to prevent tuberculosis, which was the world's first successful vaccine.
1995 Jonas Salk develops the oral polio vaccine, which greatly reduces the incidence of polio.
2006 Human papillomavirus (HPV) vaccine becomes available to prevent cervical cancer and other HPV-related cancers.
2019-2020 A novel coronavirus vaccine (COVID-19 vaccine) was developed and authorized for emergency use within a short period of time after the outbreak, becoming one of the key tools for controlling the epidemic.
